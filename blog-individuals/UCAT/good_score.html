Where To Apply With A Good UCAT Score?

15/06/20

What is a good UCAT score? Where to apply with a good UCAT score? What do universities do with a good UCAT score? How do universities use a good UCAT score? Do I need to have a good UCAT score?

What Is A Good UCAT Score?

A UCAT score that is between 650-685 is generally considered to be a good score. Please note that the specific “good” score depends on the universities you're applying for and on your personal circumstances. Indeed, what counts as a “good” score changes every year depending on the cohort’s performance.

How Do Universities Use My "Good" UCAT Score?

To build on the variation in the medical application process, different universities use the UCAT differently. For example some universities don’t have a minimum cut-off score and instead rank the applicants based on their UCAT score; other universities do have a cut-off score where you need to cross in order for your application to progress. As you will know at this point, in both cases, the higher your score is the better.

It is interesting to note that universities such as Southampton and St George’s University of London do not use the SJT.

Below we will be discussing the examples of Exeter, Southampton and St George’s University of London universities. To find out more about the university you are thinking of we recommend visiting the university’s website.

To kickstart your journey into the UCAT, sure to check out our UCAT guide. You can also access our free practice questions here.




What universities should I apply to with a good UCAT score?

Exeter University
UCAT comes secondary to the interview shortlist, but it is still important. The university initially uses predicted/achieved A-level grades to rank applicants into tiers from 1 to 4 where 1 is the highest. Achieved A*A*A* is required for a tier 1 application, predicted A*A*A* is a tier 2 application. ( You can visit the university’s website to learn more about the tiers).

All candidates in the top tier will be sinvited for an interview regardless of their UCAT score. Therefore, it is favourable to consider Exeter as an option if you already have your A-levels achieved. In other tiers, where there are too many applicants, their UCAT scores are then ranked and those with the highest UCAT scores will be invited for interviews.

Southampton University
Southampton is one of the universities that is not very transparent about its application process. However, we know that initially applicants are ranked according to their UCAT score. Then, their application is checked against the university’s own academic criteria. If the university is happy with the applicant’s performance on both counts an interview invitation is normally sent out.

As mentioned above, the university does not take into consideration the SJT score. In addition, the personal statement is not used. UCAT will not be used as part of the offermaking process. The interview performance is the only thing used to determine whether or not to be made an offer.

Even though we don’t have a lot of information about how the selection process works here. We can still deduce that a higher UCAT score will likely put you in a more privileged position.

St George's University London
Here, a minimum cut off score is used. The university is rather pedantic about this: there is an overall cut-off score, and a cut-off score for each individual subtest. These scores change every year so we recommend checking the university’s website. Moreover, an overall average of about 650-665 seems to be safe.

UCAT will not be used as part of making the offer, only for shortlisting for an interview. However, it can still be used if candidates are put on an offer waiting list. SJT is not used at all here.
