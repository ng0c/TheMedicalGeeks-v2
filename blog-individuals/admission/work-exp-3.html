How To Get Medicine Work Experience Via Email?

25/06/2020

How should I prepare for my work experience email? How should I ask for work experience in an email? What is the work experience checklist?

Introduction

This is the third and final post of our blog on getting work experience. We recommend reading the first two posts and then coming back here to read this one. You can check out the first one here and the second one here!



How To Write The Email?

We will briefly explain what you should include in the email you send to the placement providers. Then we will give you a few examples/templates to copy for your own use if you wish to.

Start off by stating who you are, what you currently do (education-wise) and then express your desire for some work experience. Then you can be more specific about what exactly you’d like to shadow.

    ✅ For example, state certain attributes about a doctor you’d like to see in action, any wards you’re interested in, any particular duty of a doctor that you’d like to observe. Finish off by stating your availability and a kind conclusion. (See Below)



Example 1

To whom it may concern,

My name is Samantha. I am a second year Biomedical Sciences student at Leeds University. I am interested in a career in medicine. I would like to have the opportunity to shadow a consultant in the respiratory ward.

I am interested in the mechanisms in breathing and have noticed that your hospital has a large respiratory ward. I am very keen to have an idea of what day-to-day life for a respiratory consultant looks like, how the interactions amongst the multidisciplinary team contribute to patient care.

In addition, I realise the importance of the leadership that doctors possess. I look forward to seeing that in action on ward rounds and in meetings.

Ideally, I can do anytime between 12 April 2021-25 June 2021. Let me know when it suits the hospital best.

Look forward to hearing from you soon.

Kind regards

Samantha


Example 2

In our first blog post we discussed a trick to increase your chances of obtaining work experience. Please make sure that you read that blog post and come back here as example 2 is specific to the trick we discussed in our first blog of that series.

Please note the medical information below are NOT necessarily accurate and are used for the sake of illustration.

Dear Dr O’brien,

My name is Christopher. I am a year 12 student at Stocktenton Sixth form College. I am interested in a future career as a cardiologist. I would like to take this opportunity to have some work experience, shadowing you and your colleagues.

I am really interested in the use of beta-blockers by diabetic people and how that can affect their blood pressure. I have done some research and I have a few particular questions on one of the mechanisms of action of beta-blockers. I have also read your paper on the contraindications of beta-blockers and why they do not apply for people with diabetes.

Moreover, I have just finished a group project on multidisciplinary teamwork in healthcare settings. This really made me want to see that work in action, outside the classroom.

I also run a personal blog where I talk about productivity and time management. I understand that as a busy consultant and researcher you have a very efficient productivity system.

As you can see, I would highly appreciate any opportunities you provide me with for work experience.

I look forward to hearing from you.

Kind regards,

Christopher



Helpful Tips And Advice

Make sure you are prepared before you make the phone call. Read the questions above again and try and be as specific as you can in your head about the sort of experience you envision having. If you feel like you need to write a script and just read it off when making the call then by all means do that.

Also, be sure that you don’t speak too slowly or quickly on the phone. If you need to, record yourself beforehand and practice until you get the pace right.

Finally, if you have any other questions about the logistics of getting to the placement or who to speak to once you get there, be sure to ask about that on the phone.
