What do the changes to the UCAT mean for my revision?

The shorter testing window means a few things

Firstly, you will have a longer time to prepare. Since testing will start later than usual you can use this time to do extra revision.

Secondly, however, since testing will end around the same time as before, you will have less flexibility in terms of when you want to take the test.

When should you take the test?

Of course, this is up to you. Moreover, we recommend that you take it towards the beginning of the summer holiday, i.e. near the beginning of the testing window.

This is because research seems to suggest that the earlier you take your UCAT test the higher your score is likely to be. For more UCAT tips check out our UCAT blog.



How will sitting the UCAT at home affect my UCAT performance?

This year you will have the option of sitting the UCAT at home. This is called UCAT Online.

Some people will prefer this if they prefer being tested in a familiar environment that they are more comfortable with. Other people, however, prefer a more formal setting in a test centre.

UCAT Online is not in anyways easier than UCAT in a testing centre. The materials are the same; a webcam and a microphone will be used to act as invigilators. Therefore, sitting the UCAT Online is only advantageous if you prefer working from home. UCAT Online is the exact same level of difficulty as UCAT in a test centre

What are the changes?

To learn more about the BMAT check out our BMAT blog.

  • Change has only been made to section 1
  • No changes have been made to sections 2 and 3
  • Section 1 will now have 32 questions instead of 35

    • This means that there will be more time available for doing fewer questions which is a welcome change.

    Section 1 questions, regardless of their format, will now have 5 different answer options: 1 correct answer and 4 incorrect answers. Previously, different questions have had different number of answer options. Many questions used to have more than 5 options. With only 5 options, it is now easier to eliminate the wrong answers when guessing. This also means you will have a higher probability of guessing the right answer. All that if you had to guess.

    There will no longer be data analysis and interference questions. These questions used to be presented in the format of a long text that is followed by many related questions. However, in the new update this will no longer be the case. All questions will be independent of each other and each question is its own. You wonâ€™t need to go back to previous questions in order to work out the solution for another one.

    This is also a welcomed update. Previously, if you found the main text for a set of questions challenging, then that could have meant that you wouldnâ€™t be able to answer the questions for all of the set. This is no longer the case. So, if a question was too challenging and you wanted to move on, you donâ€™t have to worry about it having a knock-on effect!
